What It Is

A readiness assessment that produces a profile, not just a score.

The WorkPath Assessment places you inside realistic workplace situations and reveals how you think. There are no right answers and no trick questions. The result is a personalized written profile — not a pass/fail grade, but a clear picture of where you are now and what your next growth could look like.

It sees the individual. What an entry-level analyst reveals about their thinking is different from what a senior project manager reveals — and the profile is written to each.

It's shaped to the work. Scenarios are tailored to a specific occupation, to a role inside a specific organization, or to the conditions of a specific job site.

It rests on shared definitions of success. The three dimensions draw on multiple recognized AI literacy frameworks, so the profile reflects how the field itself defines readiness.

"We're not testing what you know about AI — we're watching for how you think when AI tools are part of the picture."

How It Works

Three tiers. Each one goes deeper.

01

Baseline

Everyday Situations

Five scenarios drawn from common workplace moments — the kind where AI tools show up in ordinary work. These establish how you think about AI when it's just part of getting something done.

02

Contextualized

Role-Specific Work

Five scenarios grounded in your actual work context — the tasks, decisions, and situations you'd encounter in your specific role. The profile adapts to job type.

03

Adaptive

Goes Deeper Where It Matters

Five scenarios generated from your earlier answers — probing areas where your thinking was most revealing or where the picture needs more depth. No two profiles are identical.
