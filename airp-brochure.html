The Opportunity

AI readiness is already taking shape. The question is whether you can see it.

For the Individual

You're already developing instincts for how to work with AI — when to trust it, when to push back, how to direct it toward something useful. But without a clear picture of where you stand, you can't leverage your strengths or close your gaps with any precision. You're advancing, but you don't know how far you've come.

For the Organization

Your people are further along than you think — or they need development you haven't targeted yet. Either way, you're making AI investments without a clear view of the readiness you already have. You can't capitalize on what you can't see, and you can't develop what you can't measure.

For the Developer

Whether you're a university, a training program, or a workforce agency — your job is to build AI readiness in people. But right now you have no credible way to show where someone started, how far they've come, or what your program actually produced. The work is real. The evidence isn't.

The AI Readiness Profile makes it visible.

What It Is

A readiness profile, not a test score.

The AI Readiness Profile places respondents inside realistic workplace situations and listens for how they think. There are no right answers and no trick questions. The result is a personalized written profile — not a pass/fail grade, but a clear picture of where someone is and what their next growth looks like.

It adapts to job role and work context, so what an entry-level analyst sees is calibrated differently than what a senior project manager sees.

"We're not testing what people know about AI — we're listening for how they think when AI tools are part of the picture."

How It Works

Three tiers. Each one goes deeper.

01

Baseline

Everyday Situations

Five scenarios drawn from common workplace moments — the kind where AI tools show up in ordinary work. These establish how someone thinks about AI when it's just part of getting something done.

02

Contextualized

Role-Specific Work

Five scenarios grounded in the respondent's actual work context — the tasks, decisions, and situations they'd encounter in their specific role. The profile adapts based on job type.

03

Adaptive

Where It Gets Interesting

Five scenarios generated from the respondent's earlier answers — probing areas where their thinking was most revealing or where the picture needs more depth. No two profiles are identical.

What We Measure

Three dimensions of AI readiness.

Every response is read across three constructs. Together, they give a complete picture — not just whether someone uses AI, but how well they understand it, integrate it, and reason through it under real conditions.
